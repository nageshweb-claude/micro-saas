What are Puranas

The Living
Scripture of
Hindu Civilization

The Puranas (Sanskrit: पुराण, "ancient") are a vast genre of Indian literature that encyclopedically cover the subjects of cosmogony, cosmology, genealogies of gods, goddesses, kings, heroes, sages, and demigods. Composed primarily in Sanskrit, they have been a living tradition for thousands of years.

  • 🌌

    Pancha Lakshana

    Each Purana contains five defining characteristics: Sarga (creation), Pratisarga (recreation), Vamsha (genealogy), Manvantara (cosmic cycles), and Vamsyanucharita (royal histories).

  • ✨

    Three Traditions

    The Puranas are classified into Sattvic (Vaishnava), Rajasic (Brahma), and Tamasic (Shaiva) categories, each revealing a facet of the divine through beloved stories and wisdom.

  • 🙏

    Path of Bhakti

    The Puranas democratized Hindu philosophy by making abstract Vedic knowledge accessible through stories, metaphors, and devotional narratives that speak to all people.

धर्मः प्रोज्झितकैतवोऽत्र परमो निर्मत्सराणां सतां
वेद्यं वास्तवमत्र वस्तु शिवदं तापत्रयोन्मूलनम्

"Completely rejecting all religious activities which are materially motivated, this Bhagavata Purana propounds the highest truth, which is understandable by those devotees who are fully pure in heart. The highest truth is reality distinguished from illusion for the welfare of all."

— Śrīmad Bhāgavatam 1.1.2
